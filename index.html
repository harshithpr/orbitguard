Case study

                  
2022 Starlink Storm Incident

                

                Kp ~6
              

              

              

                SpaceX launched 49 Starlink satellites into a very low deployment orbit near 210 km on February 3, 2022. A geomagnetic storm increased atmospheric drag, and 38 satellites reentered instead of raising to operational altitude. OrbitGuard uses this as a permanent reminder that space weather is mission-critical, not decorative.

About the creator

              
Hi, I’m Harshith.

              
I’m a high school student interested in aerospace engineering, physics, mathematics, and software development. I created OrbitGuard to explore how satellite launches, space debris, and orbital congestion affect the long-term sustainability of Earth orbit.

              
My background includes AP Physics 1, Calculus, and engineering coursework, which helped me connect math and physics to real aerospace problems. OrbitGuard combines my interests in orbital mechanics, data visualization, mission design, and responsible space exploration.

              
My long-term goal is to study aerospace engineering and contribute to technologies that make spaceflight safer, smarter, and more sustainable.
